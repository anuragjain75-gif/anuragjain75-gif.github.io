## I would establish a strict visual language

Every essay thumbnail should follow the same principles:

- Monochrome (black, charcoal, and soft greys)
- Sumi-e or ink-wash style
- Minimal detail
- Plenty of empty space
- No human faces (except perhaps distant silhouettes when essential)
- No bright accents
- No text embedded in the image
- Soft paper texture rather than digital sharpness

The images should feel as though they all came from the same sketchbook.

Over a decade, readers will instantly recognize a Lilamaya essay just from the artwork.

## A wonderful long-term project

Here's an idea that I think could become one of Lilamaya's defining characteristics.

Instead of treating illustrations as decoration, we build a **curated visual library** over the years. Every illustration follows the same artistic direction, creating a coherent body of work alongside your essays. Eventually, someone could browse the illustrations alone and immediately recognize them as belonging to Lilamaya.

Since we're already using AI to create them, we can establish a master prompt that ensures remarkable consistency in style, composition, and mood. Every new essay would then receive its own bespoke ink-wash illustration, making the visual identity as enduring as the writing itself.
